> Grouped by meaning cluster so you can **compare similar forms** and feel the difference.
> Format: **Pattern** → meaning | _Example (EN)_

## CLUSTER 1 — Conditionals: "If / When"
> These four all mean "if" but with very different nuances.

| # | Pattern | Core feeling | Trigger |
|---|---------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | ～と | automatic/natural consequence | physical laws, habits |
| 2 | ～ば | hypothetical / logical | formal conditions |
| 3 | ～たら | "and then" / once that happens | sequential, personal |
| 4 | ～なら | "given that you say so" | based on new info |

**1. ～と** — _always happens, no exceptions_
> このボタンを押す**と**、ドアが開く。
> "Press this button and the door opens." (automatic)

**2. ～ば** — _logical / formal "if"_
> 毎日練習すれ**ば**、上手になる。
> "If you practice every day, you'll get better." (logical)

**3. ～たら** — _"once X, then Y" / narrative_
> 家に帰っ**たら**、すぐ連絡して。
> "Once you get home, message me right away." (sequential)

**4. ～なら** — _"if that's the case…"_
> 東京に行く**なら**、渋谷に寄ってみて。
> "If you're going to Tokyo, try stopping by Shibuya." (responding to info)

## CLUSTER 2 — Even if / Although: Concessions
> All express "despite X, Y". Differ in blame, formality, surprise.

**5. ～ても** — neutral "even if"
> 雨が降っ**ても**、試合は続ける。
> "Even if it rains, the game goes on."

**6. ～のに** — surprised/disappointed "even though"
> あんなに練習した**のに**、負けた。
> "Even though we practiced so much… we lost." (frustration)

**7. ～くせに** — blaming/sarcastic "even though"
> 何も知らない**くせに**、偉そうなことを言うな。
> "Don't act all smart when you don't know anything." (accusation)

**8. ～ながら** — "even while / despite" (formal written)
> 悪いと知り**ながら**、やめられない。
> "Even knowing it's bad, I can't stop."

**9. ～ものの** — "although…but" (acknowledges reality)
> 試してみた**ものの**、うまくいかなかった。
> "I did try, although it didn't work out."

**10. ～にもかかわらず** — formal "despite" (written/news)
> 反対意見**にもかかわらず**、計画は進んだ。
> "Despite opposition, the plan moved forward."

**11. ～とはいえ** — "that said / even so"
> 春**とはいえ**、まだ寒い日もある。
> "That said, even in spring there are cold days."

## CLUSTER 3 — Purpose / Reason: "In order to / Because"

**12. ～ために** — purpose (volitional) OR cause (non-volitional)
> 健康のため**に**、毎朝走っている。 _(purpose)_
> "I run every morning for my health."
> 事故の**ために**、電車が遅れた。 _(cause)_
> "The train was delayed because of an accident."

**13. ～ように** — purpose (for ability/state to happen)
> 忘れない**ように**、メモした。
> "I took a note so I wouldn't forget." (achieving a state)

> **KEY DIFFERENCE**: ために uses volitional verb before it; ように uses potential/negative.
> ✅ 合格するために勉強する | ✅ 合格できるように勉強する

**14. ～からには** — "now that / since (obligation follows)"
> やると決めた**からには**、最後までやる。
> "Now that I've decided to do it, I'll see it through."

**15. ～以上は (いじょうは)** — "given that / since (no backing out)"
> 引き受けた**以上は**、責任を持ってやります。
> "Since I took it on, I'll do it responsibly."

> **16 vs 15**: からには = emotional commitment; 以上は = logical obligation

**16. ～上で (うえで)** — "after doing / for the purpose of (process)"
> 内容を確認した**上で**、署名してください。
> "Please sign after confirming the contents."

## CLUSTER 4 — Speculation / Inference
> "Seems like / Looks like / Must be" — all guessing, different evidence types.

| # | Pattern | Evidence source | Certainty |
|---|---------|----------------|-----------|
| 17 | ～そうだ | visual / right now | appearance |
| 18 | ～らしい | hearsay / general | rumor/reputation |
| 19 | ～ようだ | reasoning / inference | personal judgment |
| 20 | ～はずだ | logical expectation | strong expectation |
| 21 | ～に違いない | conviction | near certainty |
| 22 | ～かもしれない | possibility | low–medium |

**17. ～そうだ** _(looks like, right now)_
> 空が暗い。雨が降り**そうだ**。
> "The sky is dark. Looks like it'll rain."

**18. ～らしい** _(I heard / apparently)_
> 彼は転職する**らしい**よ。
> "Apparently he's changing jobs." (heard from someone)

**19. ～ようだ** _(seems like, based on my reasoning)_
> 彼女は何か隠している**ようだ**。
> "It seems like she's hiding something."

**20. ～はずだ** _(should be / expected to)_
> 彼は今日来る**はずだ**。約束したから。
> "He should come today — he promised."

**21. ～に違いない** _(must be, I'm sure)_
> あんなに練習したんだから、合格に**違いない**。
> "After practicing that much, they must have passed."

**22. ～かもしれない** _(might / maybe)_
> 財布を電車に忘れてきた**かもしれない**。
> "I might have left my wallet on the train."

## CLUSTER 5 — "Only / Nothing but / Even"
> Subtle differences in restriction and nuance.

**23. ～だけ** — neutral "only"
> 少し**だけ**食べた。
> "I ate just a little."

**24. ～しか～ない** — "nothing but (implying disappointment)"
> 財布に500円**しか**ない。
> "I only have 500 yen." (frustration implied)

**25. ～ばかり** — "nothing but / just did / keeps doing"
> ゲームばかりしていないで勉強しなさい。
> "Stop doing nothing but playing games and study."
> 今来た**ばかり**です。 _(just arrived)_

**26. ～きり** — "only / just (and that's it, closed)"
> 二人き**り**で話したい。
> "I want to talk with just the two of us."

**27. ～さえ** — "even (unexpected inclusion)"
> 子供**さえ**知っている。
> "Even children know that." (minimum case proves point)

**28. ～さえ～ば** — "if only X, then Y"
> お金**さえあれ**ば、何でもできる。
> "If only I had money, I could do anything."

## CLUSTER 6 — "わけ" Family
> All use わけ (meaning/logic/situation). Easy to confuse.

**29. ～わけだ** — "that explains it / so naturally…"
> 毎日練習してたんだ。上手な**わけだ**。
> "So you were practicing every day. No wonder you're good."

**30. ～わけではない** — "it's not that… (partial negation)"
> 嫌いな**わけではない**けど、好きでもない。
> "It's not that I dislike it, but I don't love it either."

**31. ～わけがない** — "there's no way / impossible"
> 彼があんなことをする**わけがない**。
> "There's no way he'd do something like that."

**32. ～わけにはいかない** — "can't (socially/morally)"
> 友達との約束を断る**わけにはいかない**。
> "I can't back out of a promise with a friend." (obligation)

---

## CLUSTER 7 — "ところ" Family
> All use ところ (point/moment) but in very different contexts.

**33. ～たところ** — "tried and found / did and discovered"
> 電話した**ところ**、留守だった。
> "I called and found out they were out."

**34. ～ているところ** — "in the middle of doing"
> 今、ご飯を食べている**ところ**です。
> "I'm right in the middle of eating right now."

**35. ～ところが** — "however (unexpected reversal)"
> 自信があった。**ところが**、試験に落ちた。
> "I was confident. However, I failed the exam."

**36. ～ところで** — "even if (futile effort)"
> 今さら謝った**ところで**、遅すぎる。
> "Even if you apologize now, it's too late."

**37. ～ところに/へ** — "just when (timing)"
> 出かけようとした**ところに**、電話が来た。
> "Just when I was about to leave, the phone rang."

## CLUSTER 8 — "もの" Family
> All use もの (thing/fact). Soft, explanatory tone.

**38. ～ものだ** — "that's how things are / it's natural that"
> 子供は元気な**ものだ**。
> "That's just how kids are — full of energy."

**39. ～ものだから** — "because (soft explanation/excuse)"
> 道に迷った**ものだから**、遅れてしまいました。
> "Because I got lost, I ended up being late." (apologetic)

**40. ～ものの** — "although / granted that"
> 努力した**ものの**、結果は出なかった。
> "Although I made an effort, results didn't come."

**41. ～ものがある** — "there is a quality of…"
> 彼の話には心を動かす**ものがある**。
> "There's something moving about the way he talks."

**42. ～ものか** — "no way! / as if I would!" (strong refusal)
> 彼に頭を下げる**ものか**！
> "As if I'd bow my head to him!"

## CLUSTER 9 — "こと" Grammar Structures

**43. ～ことがある** — "sometimes / there are times when"
> 一人で泣く**ことがある**。
> "Sometimes I cry alone."

**44. ～ことがない** — "have never / there's no occasion"
> 海外に行った**ことがない**。
> "I've never been abroad."

**45. ～ことになる** — "it turns out / will end up (not my choice)"
> 転勤する**ことになった**。
> "It's been decided that I'll be transferred." (company decided)

**46. ～ことにする** — "I decide to (my own choice)"
> 毎日日記をつける**ことにした**。
> "I've decided to keep a journal every day."

**47. ～ことになっている** — "it is set / supposed to (rule/schedule)"
> 会議は3時から始まる**ことになっている**。
> "The meeting is scheduled to start at 3."

**48. ～ことにしている** — "I make it a rule to (habit)"
> 寝る前に本を読む**ことにしている**。
> "I make it a rule to read before bed."

**49. ～ことはない** — "no need to / never happens"
> そんなに心配する**ことはない**よ。
> "There's no need to worry so much."

## CLUSTER 10 — "ように" Structures
> The most versatile N2 pattern family.

**50. ～ようになる** — "come to do / reach the point where"
> 日本語が話せる**ようになった**。
> "I've gotten to where I can speak Japanese."

**51. ～ようにする** — "make an effort to / try to (ongoing)"
> 早く寝る**ようにしている**。
> "I try to go to bed early." (habit/effort)

**52. ～ように言う** — "tell someone to"
> 先生は静かにする**ように言った**。
> "The teacher told us to be quiet."

**53. ～ようで** — "it seems as though (soft)"
> 彼女は怒っている**ようで**、返事がない。
> "It seems she's angry — she hasn't replied."

## CLUSTER 11 — Degree / Extent

**54. ～ほど** — "to the extent that / so…that"
> 倒れる**ほど**疲れた。
> "I was tired to the point of collapsing."

**55. ～くらい/ぐらい** — "about / at least / to the degree of"
> これ**くらい**できて当然だ。
> "This much is only natural to be able to do."

**56. ～ほど～ない** — "not as…as"
> 彼**ほど**頭がいい人はいない。
> "There's no one as smart as him."

**57. ～だけ** — "as much as possible (with potential)"
> できる**だけ**早く来てください。
> "Please come as early as you can."

## CLUSTER 12 — Addition / Listing

**58. ～上に (うえに)** — "in addition to / on top of that"
> 彼女は美しい**上に**、頭もいい。
> "On top of being beautiful, she's also smart."

**59. ～だけでなく** — "not only…but also"
> 英語**だけでなく**、中国語も話せる。
> "Not only English, but also speaks Chinese."

**60. ～はもちろん** — "of course / needless to say"
> 漢字**はもちろん**、ひらがなも書ける。
> "Needless to say kanji, they can write hiragana too."

**61. ～に加えて (くわえて)** — "in addition to (formal)"
> 経験**に加えて**、資格も必要だ。
> "In addition to experience, qualifications are also needed."

**62. ～ばかりか** — "not only…but even (escalation)"
> 謝らない**ばかりか**、逆に怒り出した。
> "Not only did he not apologize, he even got angry."

**63. ～し** — "and also / what's more (multiple reasons)"
> 安い**し**、おいしい**し**、また来たい。
> "It's cheap, and it's good — I want to come again."

## CLUSTER 13 — Passive / Causative / Benefactive
> The giving/receiving/making verbs — social dynamics.

**64. ～てもらう** — "have someone do for me (favor received)"
> 友達に引っ越しを手伝って**もらった**。
> "I had my friend help me move."

**65. ～てあげる** — "do for someone (favor given)"
> 荷物を持って**あげた**。
> "I carried her luggage for her."

**66. ～てくれる** — "someone does for me (appreciation)"
> 彼が迎えに来て**くれた**。
> "He came to pick me up (for me)."

**67. ～させる** — causative "make/let someone do"
> 子供に野菜を食べ**させた**。
> "I made the child eat vegetables."

**68. ～させてもらう** — "humbly receive permission to do"
> 少し早く帰ら**せてもらえますか**。
> "Would you allow me to leave a little early?"

**69. ～させられる** — causative-passive "be made to do"
> 残業を**させられた**。
> "I was made to work overtime." (reluctantly)

## CLUSTER 14 — Tendency / Likelihood

**70. ～がちだ** — "tend to / often (negative nuance)"
> 冬は風邪をひき**がちだ**。
> "In winter, you tend to catch colds."

**71. ～やすい** — "easy to / prone to"
> この道は滑り**やすい**。
> "This road is slippery (prone to sliding)."

**72. ～にくい** — "hard to / difficult to"
> 彼の字は読み**にくい**。
> "His handwriting is hard to read."

**73. ～っぽい** — "seems like / -ish (informal tendency)"
> 彼って子供**っぽい**ね。
> "He's kind of childish, isn't he."

**74. ～気味 (ぎみ)** — "slightly / a bit (light symptom)"
> 最近、疲れ**気味**です。
> "Lately I've been feeling a bit tired."

## CLUSTER 15 — Formal Location / Relation Markers

**75. ～において** — "in / at (formal context)"
> 現代社会**において**、SNSは欠かせない。
> "In modern society, SNS is indispensable."

**76. ～に関して (かんして)** — "regarding / concerning"
> この件**に関して**、調査中です。
> "We are currently investigating regarding this matter."

**77. ～について** — "about / concerning (everyday)"
> 環境問題**について**話し合った。
> "We discussed about environmental issues."

**78. ～によって** — "by / due to / depending on (varies)"
> 文化**によって**、考え方が違う。
> "Ways of thinking differ depending on culture."

**79. ～に対して (たいして)** — "toward / against / compared to"
> 彼は私**に対して**冷たい。
> "He's cold toward me."

**80. ～として** — "as / in the capacity of"
> 医者**として**、アドバイスします。
> "As a doctor, I'll give you advice."

**81. ～をめぐって** — "surrounding (a controversy)"
> その法案**をめぐって**議論が続いている。
> "Debate continues surrounding that bill."

**82. ～をもとに** — "based on"
> 実話**をもとに**した映画だ。
> "It's a movie based on a true story."

**83. ～を通して (とおして)** — "through / throughout"
> 音楽**を通して**、友達になった。
> "We became friends through music."

**84. ～に沿って (そって)** — "along / in accordance with"
> 計画**に沿って**進める。
> "Proceed in accordance with the plan."

## CLUSTER 16 — Time Sequences

**85. ～次第 (しだい)** — "as soon as (formal)"
> 準備でき**次第**、出発します。
> "We'll depart as soon as preparations are done."

**86. ～た途端に (とたんに)** — "the moment / the instant"
> 外に出た**途端に**、雨が降り出した。
> "The moment I stepped outside, it started raining."

**87. ～てから** — "after doing (then)"
> シャワーを浴び**てから**、寝る。
> "After taking a shower, I'll sleep."

**88. ～た後で (あとで)** — "after (completed action)"
> 宿題をした**後で**、ゲームしていい。
> "After you finish homework, you can play games."

**89. ～うちに** — "while / before it changes"
> 若い**うちに**、いろんな経験をすべきだ。
> "You should have various experiences while you're young."

**90. ～間に (あいだに)** — "during / within a time window"
> 留守の**間に**、泥棒が入った。
> "A thief broke in while I was away."

## CLUSTER 17 — Can't / Won't / Impossible

**91. ～かねる** — "unable to / reluctant to (polite refusal)"
> その件についてはお答え**しかねます**。
> "I'm afraid I cannot answer regarding that matter."

**92. ～かねない** — "might / could (negative risk)"
> 無理をすると体を壊し**かねない**。
> "If you push yourself, you could end up ruining your health."

**93. ～得る (うる/える)** — "can / possible"
> そういうことは起こり**得る**。
> "That kind of thing can happen."

**94. ～得ない (えない)** — "cannot / impossible"
> そんなことは**あり得ない**。
> "That's impossible. / No way."

## CLUSTER 18 — Should / Ought to / Expected

**95. ～べきだ** — "should / ought to (moral)"
> 困っている人を助ける**べきだ**。
> "You ought to help people in trouble."

**96. ～べきではない** — "should not"
> 嘘をつく**べきではない**。
> "You shouldn't lie."

**97. ～はずだ** — "should be (logical expectation)" _(see #20)_

**98. ～ないといけない** — "have to / must (casual)"
> もう行か**ないといけない**。
> "I have to go now."

**100. ～ばよかった** — "should have / wish I had"

### "So that / In order to" Choice Guide
```
volitional action for a goal  →  ～ために
achieve a state / ability     →  ～ように
after completing a process    →  ～上で
```

### "Since / Now that" Commitment Level
```
mild logical reason   →  ～から
stronger commitment   →  ～からには
formal/written        →  ～以上は
```

### Speculation Certainty Scale
```
低                                              高
かもしれない → ようだ → らしい → はずだ → に違いない
maybe         seems    heard    expected  must be
```

## STUDY STRATEGY

1. **Week 1–2**: Master Clusters 1, 4, 9 (conditionals + speculation + こと patterns) — highest test frequency
2. **Week 3**: わけ family (#29–32) and ように family (#50–53) — often confused on N2
3. **Week 4**: Formal markers (#75–84) — appear heavily in reading sections
4. **Ongoing**: Use the comparison tables above when you mix up similar forms
5. **Recall trick**: For each grammar, remember one sentence that *feels* real to you — the "vibe" sentence above is a starting point, replace with your own experience
